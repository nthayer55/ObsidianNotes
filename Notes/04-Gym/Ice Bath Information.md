
## Effective Temperature Ranges

Temperatures can range from $40-60 \degree F$ where the warmer the water, the longer exposure required. Bias is towards the $45-50 \degree F$ range.

## Recommended Exposure Length

Target 11 minutes of total exposure over the week in 1-5 minute sessions based on temperature. 

## Sanitation

*Target Volume: 110 Gallons | Sanitizer: 12% Food-Grade $H_{2}O_{2}$*
### 1. Dosing Guide
To maintain the target sanitizing range of **40–60 ppm**:

| Action | Dosage Amount | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| **Initial Fill / Shock** | 1.5 to 2 cups (12–16 oz) | Fresh water fill or if water turns cloudy |
| **Weekly Maintenance** | 1/4 to 1/2 cup (2–4 oz) | Once per week (scale up if plunging daily) |
### 2. Execution Steps
 1. **Test:** Dip a high-level $H_2O_2$ test strip. Aim for 40–60 ppm. Skip dosing if it reads above 30 ppm.
 2. **Pour:** Pour the dose directly in front of the water return jet while the pump is running.
 3. **Circulate:** Let the pump run for **30 minutes** before plunging to ensure even distribution.
### 3. Key Rules & Maintenance
 * **Storage:** Keep the 12% bottle in a cool, dark place. Light and heat degrade it into plain water.
 * **Filter Care:** Check and rinse the filter cartridge weekly. Peroxide oxidizes organic waste, which quickly traps in the filter.
 * **The "Reset" Dose:** If the water becomes dull or smells musty, shock the tub with **3 cups** of 12% $H_2O_2$, run the pump for 2 hours, and clean the filter.
